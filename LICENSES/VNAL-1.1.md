## 1. Purpose

This license governs systems, implementations, simulators, and runtime structures that are based on, inspired by, or related to VECTAETOS™ concepts.

Its purpose is to ensure that implementation systems remain non-agentic, non-authoritative, non-optimizing, uncertainty-preserving, and explicitly separated from the canonical ontology.

## 2. Covered Systems

This license applies to ASIMULATOR-like systems, simulation engines, runtime structures, dialogue prototypes, output-producing implementations, trajectory exploration tools, adapter-integrated prototypes, and systems exposing traces, projections, or responses.

Canonical ontology remains governed by VCL-2.0. Software tooling may also be governed by AEPL-2.0-VECTAETOS.

## 3. Definitions

**Non-agentic** — not optimizing, not selecting preferred states, not ranking trajectories, not implementing goal-seeking behavior, and not exercising autonomous authority.

**Non-authoritative** — not claiming final truth, not issuing binding decisions, and not collapsing epistemic plurality into a single enforced answer.

**Epistemic plurality** — structural coexistence of multiple epistemic states, including uncertainty and QE where applicable.

**QE / aporia** — legitimate non-representability or unresolved boundary state.

**Silence** — legitimate non-output where output would force false closure.

## 4. Permissions

You may use, modify, distribute, and study covered systems for non-authoritative research and experimentation.

## 5. Required Conditions

Any derivative or redistributed system must:

1. retain this license or a clear reference to it;
2. state modifications;
3. preserve explicit uncertainty where the system is designed to expose it;
4. preserve QE, aporia, or silence where structurally required;
5. avoid representing outputs as factual certainty without qualification;
6. avoid representing itself as canonical VECTAETOS™ unless authorized;
7. clearly distinguish implementation behavior from ontology;
8. preserve non-agentic and non-authoritative framing.

## 6. Strict Prohibitions

You may not:

1. collapse output into a single mandatory answer where plurality is required;
2. rank or select a “best” answer as VECTAETOS™ truth;
3. remove or hide uncertainty annotations;
4. use the system for decision-making automation;
5. integrate covered systems with reward-based optimization loops that destroy non-agentic structure;
6. represent outputs as factual certainty without uncertainty;
7. force output where silence, QE, or aporia is structurally valid;
8. make the implementation mutate Φ, canonical anchors, or protected ontology;
9. claim that the implementation validates deployment;
10. claim that the implementation proves safety;
11. claim that the implementation is an official VECTAETOS™ agent;
12. use the system as a covert authority layer for coercive, punitive, manipulative, repressive, military, targeting, or surveillance systems.

## 7. ASIMULATOR Boundary

An ASIMULATOR-like system may explore procedural possibility.

It may not claim to be a valid implementation of VECTAETOS™ if it introduces memory influence into Φ, adaptive feedback into the ontological core, learning mechanisms that redefine canonical boundaries, decision authority, reward loops, or best-trajectory selection.

Such systems may remain useful as external experiments, but they are separate lineages unless the non-agentic boundary is preserved.
