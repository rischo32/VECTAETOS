## 1. Purpose

This license governs projection artifacts and structural trace artifacts associated with VECTAETOS™.

Its purpose is to preserve the distinction between structural exposure and interpretation authority.

```text
projection ≠ interpretation
glyph ≠ verdict
rune ≠ command
hash ≠ truth
ledger ≠ ontology
EK trace ≠ deployment validation
```

## 2. Covered Artifacts

This license applies to TetraGlyph artifacts, glyphs, runes, projection diagrams, symbolic renderings, EK observables, audit traces, structural fingerprints, hash reports, Merkle roots, ledger-like records, trace bundles, and projection metadata.

It does not automatically override licenses for external images, datasets, or publications that declare separate terms.

## 3. Permissions

You may study, display, cite, discuss, redistribute, and create derivative visual or symbolic renderings of covered projection artifacts, provided they remain non-authoritative.

## 4. Required Conditions

Redistribution or derivative use must:

1. retain attribution;
2. state modifications;
3. preserve the distinction between projection and interpretation;
4. avoid representing glyphs, runes, hashes, or traces as truth proofs;
5. avoid representing EK observables as decisions, safety scores, deployment validators, or ontology validators;
6. preserve uncertainty-bearing and non-final language where applicable;
7. distinguish symbolic representation from canonical ontology.

## 5. Strict Prohibitions

You may not:

1. represent a projection artifact as an official interpretation of Φ unless authorized;
2. represent a glyph or rune as a decision, instruction, command, ranking, recommendation, or verdict;
3. represent a hash, signature, fingerprint, Merkle root, or ledger entry as semantic truth;
4. represent EK observables as safety validation, deployment approval, empirical proof, or ontology proof;
5. use projection artifacts to create a scoring, ranking, or targeting system;
6. remove uncertainty or aporia indicators from artifacts that structurally require them;
7. claim that symbolic output replaces human judgment, review, or interpretation.

## 6. EK Trace Boundary

Epistemic Cryptography traces may expose structural coherence, asymmetry, uncertainty, or integrity drift.

They do not decide, optimize, intervene, rank, recommend, validate deployment, prove truth, or mutate Φ.
