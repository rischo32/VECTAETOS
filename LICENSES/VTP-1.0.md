## 1. Purpose

This policy governs use of VECTAETOS™ names, marks, identifiers, badges, compatibility claims, and public identity.

Its purpose is to protect the identity, integrity, and non-agentic meaning of VECTAETOS™ while allowing fair reference, research discussion, citation, educational use, compatible community work, and clearly separated downstream research.

## 2. Covered Marks

This policy covers VECTAETOS™, VECTAETΦS™, VECTAETOS, VECTAETΦS, VECTAETOS Framework, VECTAETOS Project, VECTAETOS FOUNDATIONS, VECTLAB™, VECTLAB Research Node, VECTLAB Epistemic Shield, related logos, glyph marks, badges, favicons, repository identifiers, domains, package names, and confusingly similar identifiers.

Use of code, documentation, or public materials does not grant ownership of the marks.

## 3. Ontological Identity Clause

VECTAETOS™ is a non-agentic onto-epistemic field framework.

The marks must not be used to represent VECTAETOS™ as:

```text
an autonomous agent
a decision system
an optimization mechanism
a recommendation engine
a regulatory authority
a deployment validator
a truth authority
a safety guarantee
a productized AGI system
a coercive operational layer
a weapon system
a targeting system
a surveillance system
a military command system
```

Correct reading:

```text
VECTAETOS is the non-agentic epistemic field framework.
VECTLAB is a downstream research node.
Neither owns Φ.
Neither decides truth.
```

## 4. Permitted Use Without Prior Written Permission

You may use the marks for limited, truthful, non-confusing reference.

Allowed:

```text
This paper discusses VECTAETOS™.
This repository experiments with concepts inspired by VECTAETOS™.
This implementation is not affiliated with or endorsed by VECTAETOS™.
Compatible with selected VECTAETOS™ concepts; not an official implementation.
```

Such use must not imply endorsement, certification, partnership, canonical status, deployment validation, defense readiness, or decision authority.

## 5. Uses Requiring Prior Written Permission

Prior written permission is required to use covered marks in product names, service names, company names, organization names, foundation names, domain names, package names, repository names, GitHub organizations, commercial training, certification labels, badges implying official status, fundraising or grant materials implying endorsement, research-node status claims, deployment validation claims, or defense capability claims.

## 6. Prohibited Uses

You may not use covered marks in a way that suggests official status, endorsement, certification, project ownership, authority to define canonical VECTAETOS meaning, authority to validate deployments, commercial representation authority, or operational VECTLAB authority where none exists.

You may not use confusing names such as:

```text
vectaetos-agent
vectaetos-optimizer
vectaetos-decision-system
vectaetos-safety-score
vectaetos-certified
official-vectaetos
vectlab-defense
vectlab-command
vectlab-targeting
vectlab-certified
```

## 7. Compatibility Claims

Compatibility claims are allowed only if accurate and non-confusing.

Allowed:

```text
compatible with selected VECTAETOS™ concepts
uses terminology from the VECTAETOS™ framework
inspired by VECTAETOS™
unofficial implementation referencing VECTAETOS™ boundaries
```

Not allowed without permission:

```text
VECTAETOS-certified
official VECTAETOS-compatible
VECTAETOS validated
VECTLAB certified
deployment-ready under VECTAETOS
VECTAETOS safety approved
```

Compatibility does not imply endorsement, canonical status, or safety.

Allowed examples:

Not allowed without permission:

```text

Official VECTAETOS
```

## 9. VECTLAB Boundary

VECTLAB™ Research Node may refer to downstream applied epistemic-security research.

It must not be represented as a weapon system, targeting system, autonomous agent, truth classifier, safety-certified product, operational defense product, or deployment authority.

Canonical sentence:

```text
VECTLAB is a research node, not an authority.
```

Slovensky:

```text
VECTLAB je výskumný uzol, nie autorita.
```


Prohibited framing:

```text
weaponization

operational military command

battlefield decision automation
guaranteed defense capability
```

```text
Defensive research and protective documentation hardening are admissible.
Weaponization, targeting, attack, surveillance, and operational command are not.
```

## 11. Enforcement

If a use violates this policy, the project owner may request correction of attribution, removal of the mark, renaming, removal of misleading claims, removal of confusing badges, public clarification that the use is unofficial, or clarification that no endorsement, certification, deployment validation, defense readiness, or decision authority is implied.

Failure to correct misuse may result in further legal or platform-level action.

Failure to enforce this policy in one case does not waive the right to enforce it in another case.

Permission for one use does not imply permission for another use.
