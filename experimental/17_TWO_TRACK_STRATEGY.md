# Two-Track Strategy — Epistemic Cryptography vs VECTAETOS

Status: Draft  
Purpose: Keep the general framework independent from the specific ontology.

---

## Track A — Epistemic Cryptography

### Identity

General structural audit framework.

### Scope

Any finite relational system with:

- nodes
- relations
- uncertainty
- asymmetry
- structural coherence

### Claims

Can claim:

```text
read-only audit
structural coherence
uncertainty geometry
non-interventional diagnostics
```

Must not require:

- 8 singularities
- VECTAETOS ontology
- ZMYSEL
- QE
- triality

### Audience

- AI safety researchers
- systems theorists
- audit / verification researchers
- complexity researchers

### Strongest claim

```text
Epistemic Cryptography protects structural coherence rather than content secrecy.
```

---

## Track B — VECTAETOS

### Identity

Specific non-agentic epistemic field ontology.

### Scope

Uses:

- 8 invariant singularities
- so(8)
- Δ curvature
- 𝒟 admissible domain
- ZMYSEL carrier
- QE
- OAAT

### Claims

Can claim:

```text
formal ontology of representability
non-agentic relational field
candidate safety-relevant architecture
```

Must not claim:

```text
validated general AI safety
```

### Audience

- formal philosophy
- AI safety architecture
- mathematical systems theory
- epistemology

---

## Why Separation Matters

If VECTAETOS is rejected as too ontological, Epistemic Cryptography can still stand as a structural audit method.

If Epistemic Cryptography is accepted, VECTAETOS can be presented as one specialized architecture that uses it.

---

## Recommended Public Strategy

Lead with Epistemic Cryptography for general audiences.

Use VECTAETOS for deeper architecture.

Suggested hierarchy:

```text
Epistemic Cryptography — general audit framework
VECTAETOS — ontological field architecture
ASIMULATOR / ASI_MOD — downstream experimental layers
```

Epistemic Cryptography can be used without VECTAETOS.

VECTAETOS may use Epistemic Cryptography, but is not identical to it.

Do not collapse them.
