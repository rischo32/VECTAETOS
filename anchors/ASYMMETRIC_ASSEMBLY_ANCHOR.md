# ASYMMETRIC ASSEMBLY ANCHOR
## VECTAETOS™ / ASIMULATOR™ / ASI_MOD™
### Canonical Dependency-and-Assembly Anchor
### Status: CANONICAL
### Ontological Role: Assembly Invariant

---

## 0. PURPOSE

This anchor defines the asymmetric dependency structure of the triadic architecture:

- VECTAETOS™
- ASIMULATOR™
- ASI_MOD™

Its purpose is not merely to connect repositories,
but to define under what conditions they are ontologically valid,
architecturally complete,
and operationally admissible.

---

## 1. PRIMARY CONDITION

VECTAETOS is the ontological condition of the architecture.

This means:

- VECTAETOS may exist without ASIMULATOR
- VECTAETOS may exist without ASI_MOD
- VECTAETOS remains ontologically valid without both

But:

- ASIMULATOR may not claim valid standalone existence without VECTAETOS
- ASI_MOD may not claim valid standalone existence without VECTAETOS

---

## 2. SECONDARY NON-SELF-SUFFICIENCY

ASIMULATOR and ASI_MOD are modular parts,
but neither is self-sufficient.

This means:

- ASIMULATOR alone is invalid as an architectural layer
- ASI_MOD alone is invalid as an architectural layer
- ASIMULATOR + ASI_MOD without VECTAETOS is invalid
- neither module may claim full-layer legitimacy in isolation

They are partial modules,
not independent systems.

---

## 3. ASSEMBLY CONDITION

The triadic assembly is complete only if all three are present:

VECTAETOS + ASIMULATOR + ASI_MOD

However, presence alone is not enough.

Complete presence defines structural assembly,
but not yet operative legitimacy.

---

## 4. EMPIRICAL SAFETY GATE

Even if all three repositories are present,
no higher operative legitimacy exists
until VECTAETOS is empirically verified
as a safe epistemic space for more than foundational use.

Therefore:

- assembly may be structurally complete
- but remain operationally suspended

If empirical safety is not established,
the upper layers remain bounded and non-legitimized.

---

## 5. VALIDITY STATES

### 5.1 Foundational Validity
VECTAETOS alone = valid

### 5.2 Partial Invalidity
ASIMULATOR alone = invalid
ASI_MOD alone = invalid
ASIMULATOR + ASI_MOD = invalid without VECTAETOS

### 5.3 Structural Completeness
VECTAETOS + ASIMULATOR + ASI_MOD = structurally complete

### 5.4 Operative Admissibility
VECTAETOS + ASIMULATOR + ASI_MOD + empirical safety verification = conditionally admissible

---

## 6. NO INVERSE NECESSITY

The upper layers depend on VECTAETOS.
VECTAETOS does not depend on the upper layers.

This dependency is ontologically asymmetric and irreversible.

Any attempt to define VECTAETOS through ASIMULATOR or ASI_MOD invalidates the architecture.

---

## 7. MECHANIZATION PRINCIPLE

This anchor must be mechanized through:

- canonical assembly manifest owned by VECTAETOS
- signed anchor reference and lock hashes
- boot-time dependency attestation
- fail-closed startup behavior in ASIMULATOR and ASI_MOD
- no valid runtime mode for either upper layer without assembly verification

---

## 8. FAILURE MODES

The architecture fails if:

- ASIMULATOR claims standalone validity
- ASI_MOD claims standalone validity
- both upper layers operate without VECTAETOS
- upper layers bypass assembly verification
- VECTAETOS becomes dependent on its own derivatives
- structural assembly is mistaken for empirical safety

---

## 9. CANONICAL SENTENCE

VECTAETOS is the ontological condition of the architecture.
ASIMULATOR and ASI_MOD are non-self-sufficient modules.
They become structurally complete only together,
and become operatively admissible only after empirical safety verification of VECTAETOS.
