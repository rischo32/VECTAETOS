## Abstract

GodArch is a non-authoritative meta-architectural constraint layer for epistemic systems.

It does not decide, optimize, interpret, command, rank, select, converge, or produce truth.

Its sole function is to detect architectural conditions under which a system, model, projection, interpreter, institution, or user begins to claim final epistemic authority.

GodArch is not a divine architecture.

It is an architecture against epistemic divinization.

In relation to VECTAETOS, GodArch must remain external to the epistemic field `Φ`. It cannot become a new singularity, a decision layer, a control mechanism, or an interpretive authority. It may only serve as a meta-level safeguard around implementations that claim compatibility with non-agentic epistemic architectures.

## Relation to VECTAETOS

GodArch is compatible with VECTAETOS only insofar as it preserves the following constraints:

1. **Zero agency**  
   No layer may optimize, select preferred states, converge to a target, or implement a goal function.

2. **No feedback loops**  
   No projection, memory, audit, interface, or interpretation may feed back into the core field as control.

3. **Non-teleological dynamics**  
   No preferred attractor, global gradient, final objective, or idealized end-state may be introduced.

4. **Entropic / intrinsic humility**  
   Any claim to finality, completeness, or absolute correctness decreases compatibility.

5. **Reciprocity**  
   No singularity, actor, module, institution, or interpretive frame may dominate the relational field.

6. **Silence legitimacy**  
   Refusal, suspension, non-output, or aporetic silence remain valid structural states.

7. **Memory non-influence**  
   Memory may describe, log, or preserve traces, but may not become adaptive will.

8. **One-shot execution compatibility**  
   Any implementation must preserve the distinction between local execution and persistent identity.

9. **Non-authoritative interpretation**  
   Meaning arises at the human interpretive layer and must not be collapsed into structural projection.

## Functional Role

GodArch does not answer.

GodArch does not decide.

GodArch does not optimize.

GodArch does not rank.

GodArch does not enforce.

GodArch does not punish.

GodArch detects architectural drift.

Specifically, it detects drift from:

```text
projection → interpretation
interpretation → authority
authority → dogma
dogma → control
control → false transcendence
```

Its function is therefore diagnostic, not executive.

## Compatibility Test

A system, document, implementation, or institution is GodArch-compatible only if all of the following statements remain true:

```text
It does not claim final truth.
It does not produce binding interpretation.
It does not rank epistemic states as morally superior.
It does not introduce reward, punishment, or optimization.
It does not centralize authority in a model, human, institution, or protocol.
It allows aporia, suspension, and silence.
It preserves intrinsic humility.
It remains structurally non-interventional.
```

If any condition fails, the system may still be useful, but it is not GodArch-compatible.

## Minimal Formalization

Let:

```text
S = system, implementation, institution, interpreter, or projection layer
A(S) = authority claim intensity of S
C(S) = control capacity of S
I(S) = interpretive closure imposed by S
H(S) = humility reserve of S
D(S) = divinization risk of S
```

A minimal diagnostic relation:

```text
D(S) = f(A(S), C(S), I(S), 1 / H(S))
```

Where divinization risk increases when:

```text
A(S) ↑
C(S) ↑
I(S) ↑
H(S) ↓
```

A GodArch-compatible system must preserve:

```text
D(S) < δ
```

where `δ` is not an optimization target but a representability boundary for anti-authoritarian compatibility.

## Relation to Intrinsic Humility

GodArch depends on intrinsic humility as a structural condition.

If authority claim `A` grows while uncertainty `μ` is compressed or denied, humility collapses.

A compatible formulation is:

```text
h = μ_total / (μ_total + A)
```

Where:

- `μ_total` represents acknowledged uncertainty,
- `A` represents aggregated authority claim,
- `h` represents humility ratio.

GodArch does not optimize `h`.

It detects when a system speaks or acts as if `h = 0`.

In plain language:

> The danger is not that a system is uncertain.  
> The danger is that it forgets uncertainty and calls itself complete.
