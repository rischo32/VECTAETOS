# VECTAETOS — Representability Constraint

Status: Research Layer  
Layer: Structural Constraint  
Scope: Condition of epistemic realizability  

---

## 0. Purpose

This document defines the representability constraint of the epistemic field.

Representability determines whether a given field configuration can exist
as a coherent relational structure.

This constraint does not evaluate truth, correctness, or utility.

It defines structural admissibility.

---

## 1. Definition

Let:

Φ = (Σ, R)

be the epistemic field.

Define:

K(Φ) ∈ {true, false}

Where:

- K(Φ) = true → field is representable  
- K(Φ) = false → field is non-representable  

---

## 2. Condition of Representability

A field Φ is representable if there exists a mapping:

ρ : Σ → V

such that:

R(i,j) = f(ρ(i), ρ(j))

for some antisymmetric function f.

If no such mapping exists:

K(Φ) = false

---

## 3. Relation to Coherence

Representability is necessary but not sufficient for admissibility.

A valid epistemic field must satisfy:

K(Φ) = true  
C(Φ) ≥ κ  

Thus:

- K(Φ) ensures structural consistency  
- C(Φ) ensures relational coherence  

---

## 4. Non-Representable States

If:

K(Φ) = false

then:

- the field cannot be embedded in any representation space  
- relational structure loses interpretability  
- projection becomes undefined  

This condition is not an error.

It is a structural boundary.

---

## 5. Relation to QE

Qualitative Epistemic Aporia (QE) occurs when:

no admissible trajectory preserves representability and coherence.

Formally:

¬∃ τ such that:

K(Φ + τ) = true  
C(Φ + τ) ≥ κ  

QE represents:

- loss of representable trajectories  
- boundary of epistemic space  

---

## 6. Role in Simulation

The Simulation Vortex generates candidate deformations:

Φ → Φ′

These candidates are not evaluated internally.

Representability is applied externally as a constraint:

- non-representable states are not admissible  
- admissible states form the epistemic space  

---

## 7. Relation to 3Gate

The 3Gate layer operates prior to Φ.

Its role is structural shaping of input.

3Gate does not determine K(Φ).

It ensures that input does not collapse before entering Φ.

Representability is evaluated only at the level of the field.

---

## 8. Implementation Perspective

In runtime systems:

- K(Φ) is not approximated by heuristics  
- no scoring or classification is used  
- no decision boundary is implemented  

Representability is a structural condition, not an algorithm.

---

## 9. Ontological Constraint

There is no mapping:

K(Φ) → action  

The system does not:

- reject inputs  
- modify trajectories  
- enforce outcomes  

Representability is descriptive, not prescriptive.

---

## 10. Summary

Representability defines the boundary of structural existence.

It separates:

- admissible relational configurations  
- non-realizable structures  

It does not:

- evaluate meaning  
- define correctness  
- introduce decision logic  

Vectaetos does not decide.

It reveals whether structure can exist.
