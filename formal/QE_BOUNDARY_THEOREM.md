# VECTAETOS — QE Boundary Theorem

Status: OFFICIAL  
Ontology: FROZEN  
Lineage: 1.x  
Scope: Ontological Boundary of Epistemic Space

This document defines the structural origin of **QE — Qualitative Epistemic Aporia**.

QE is not an error condition.

QE represents the **topological boundary of the epistemic space E**.

---

# 1. Epistemic Space

Let the epistemic field be defined as:

Φ = (Σ, R)

where:

Σ = {Σ₁ … Σ₈}  
R ∈ so(8)

The admissible epistemic space is:

E = { Φ | K(Φ) = 1 }

where:

K(Φ) is the representability predicate.

---

# 2. Coherence Condition

Representability requires that coherence remains above the ontological threshold κ.

Formally:

K(Φ) = 1 ⇔ H(Φ) ≥ κ

where:

H(Φ) = coherence measure  
κ = representability threshold

Configurations with H(Φ) < κ are not representable.

---

# 3. Graph Representation of Φ

Let the relational structure of Φ be represented as a graph:

G(Φ)

where:

Vertices = Σᵢ  
Edges = Rᵢⱼ ≠ 0

The relational field is therefore equivalent to a tension graph.

---

# 4. Field Fragmentation

Define a subset:

C ⊂ Σ

If the following condition holds:

∀ i ∈ C, ∀ j ∉ C : Rᵢⱼ = 0

then the field separates into disconnected components.

This represents **topological fragmentation of Φ**.

---

# 5. Definition of QE

QE (Qualitative Epistemic Aporia) occurs when the epistemic field exits the representable region of epistemic space.

Formally:

QE occurs if:

K(Φ) = 0

or equivalently:

H(Φ) < κ

or when relational fragmentation prevents global coherence.

---

# 6. Structural Interpretation

QE is not:

- an error
- a failure
- a trigger for correction

QE represents a **boundary condition of epistemic space**.

Beyond QE, relational configurations cannot sustain coherent representation.

---

# 7. Consequences

The existence of QE implies:

1. Epistemic space is bounded.
2. Not all relational configurations are representable.
3. Some trajectories of relational deformation leave the epistemic space.

QE therefore functions as a **structural horizon of the epistemic field**.

---

# 8. Canonical Statement

QE is the topological boundary of the representable epistemic space.

It arises when relational deformation destroys global coherence or fragments the relational topology of the field.

QE therefore preserves the ontological integrity of Φ by marking the limit of representable configurations.
