VECTAETOS — COHERENCE THRESHOLD κ

Status: CANONICAL FORMAL EXTENSION  
Layer: Ontological Constraint  
Scope: Boundary of Epistemic Realizability  

---

0. Purpose

This document defines κ (kappa), the coherence threshold of the epistemic field Φ.

κ is not a tunable parameter, not a metric, and not a target.

κ defines the boundary between:

- representable epistemic configurations
- non-representable states (QE)

---

1. Definition

Let:

K(Φ) = global coherence predicate

Then:

κ is the minimal ontological condition such that:

K(Φ) ≥ κ  → Φ is admissible  
K(Φ) < κ  → Φ enters QE  

κ is therefore not a value, but a boundary condition on K(Φ).

---

2. Nature of κ

κ is:

- non-numeric
- non-optimizable
- non-adjustable
- invariant across all states

κ is not computed.

κ is revealed only through failure of representability.

---

3. Relationship to Coherence

Given:

C(Φ) ∈ [0,1]

C(Φ) approximates structural coherence.

However:

C(Φ) ≠ K(Φ)

K(Φ) is a predicate:

K(Φ) = true  → representable  
K(Φ) = false → non-representable  

κ is the boundary at which:

C(Φ) can no longer guarantee K(Φ)

---

4. κ as Ontological Boundary

κ separates:

E = { Φ | K(Φ) = true }

from:

QE = { Φ | K(Φ) = false }

Thus:

κ = ∂E

κ is the boundary of the epistemic space.

---

5. Emergence of QE

QE occurs when:

¬∃ representation ρ such that:

R(i,j) = f(ρ(i), ρ(j))

This implies:

K(Φ) = false

Thus:

Φ ∈ QE

This transition is not gradual.

It is a structural failure of representability.

---

6. κ and Simulation Vortex

The Simulation Vortex generates candidate trajectories:

Φ → Φ′

The Vortex has no access to κ.

After generation:

K(Φ′) is evaluated externally.

If:

K(Φ′) ≥ κ → trajectory admissible  
K(Φ′) < κ → trajectory rejected (QE)

Thus κ is never used as a control signal.

---

7. Non-Intervention Property

κ does not:

- guide trajectories
- influence generation
- optimize behavior

κ only defines:

whether a state can exist in the epistemic space.

---

8. Observability

κ is not directly observable.

It is detected only through:

- failure of representation
- collapse of projection
- emergence of QE

Thus κ is an inferred boundary.

---

9. Conceptual Interpretation

κ is the ontological limit of knowledge structure.

It ensures that:

- incoherent configurations cannot stabilize
- destructive epistemic states do not persist
- the field remains structurally consistent

---

10. Summary

κ is not a parameter.

κ is the boundary of existence for epistemic configurations.

Vectaetos does not optimize coherence.

It reveals whether coherence is sufficient to exist.

---

No control loops  

κ is not used.  
κ is encountered.
