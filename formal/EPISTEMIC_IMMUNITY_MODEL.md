# VECTAETOS — EPISTEMIC IMMUNITY MODEL

## Purpose

This document defines the **Epistemic Immunity Model** of VECTAETOS.

Unlike conventional AI systems, Vectaetos does not implement security
through rules, restrictions, filtering layers, or enforcement mechanisms.

Security emerges as a **structural property of the epistemic field Φ**.

The system is not protected by control.

The system is protected by **non-representability of destructive states**.

---

# Core Principle

Security is not enforced.

Security is the **impossibility of certain configurations existing**.

In Vectaetos, harmful or destabilizing trajectories do not need to be detected,
blocked, or penalized.

They simply **cannot become stable states of the field Φ**.

---

# Ontological Constraint

Let:

Φ = epistemic field  
K(Φ) = global coherence predicate  
κ = minimal ontological coherence boundary  

Any configuration that would produce:

K(Φ) < κ

cannot form a stable representable transition.

Therefore:

Such configurations **never become realizable trajectories**.

# From Security to Immunity

Traditional security systems operate through:

- rule enforcement

Vectaetos does none of these.

Epistemic Immunity.

# Definition — Epistemic Immunity

Epistemic Immunity is the structural property of an epistemic field
in which destabilizing impulses fail to form representable trajectories.

This occurs when:

1. a transition would collapse global coherence
2. a trajectory would increase systemic entropy beyond recoverability
3. a configuration violates the epistemic topology of the field

In such cases:

the transition **does not exist** within the representable state space.

Entropic Humility is the ontological property that guarantees
no intelligence possesses complete knowledge of the field.

systems cannot confidently act on incomplete epistemic structures.

Entropic Humility therefore acts as the **first layer of immunity**.

Not through prohibition,
but through **structural epistemic incompleteness**.
