## 0. Purpose

This document defines the regulatory positioning of **VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core** in relation to Regulation (EU) 2024/1689, the EU Artificial Intelligence Act.

The purpose of this statement is to clarify that VECTAETOS™ 1.x, as formally defined in its frozen ontological lineage, is not designed, structured, or operated as:

- an AI system,
- a decision system,
- a recommendation engine,
- an autonomous agent,
- an optimization mechanism,
- a profiling system,
- a ranking system,
- a behavioural control mechanism,
- a conformity assessment body,
- a legal compliance certification mechanism,
- or an operational authority.

This assessment applies strictly to the frozen VECTAETOS™ 1.x ontological core.

It does **not** automatically apply to downstream, future, experimental, procedural, simulation, audit, interface, commercial, SaaS, API, LLM-based, third-party, or deployment layers.

## 3. Regulatory Reference: EU AI Act Definition of AI System

Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 defines an AI system as a machine-based system designed to operate with varying levels of autonomy, which may exhibit adaptiveness after deployment, and which, for explicit or implicit objectives, infers from input how to generate outputs such as predictions, content, recommendations, or decisions that can influence physical or virtual environments.

For positioning purposes, the relevant definitional elements are:

1. machine-based system,
2. varying levels of autonomy,
3. adaptiveness after deployment,
4. inference from input,
5. explicit or implicit objectives,
6. generation of outputs such as predictions, content, recommendations, or decisions,
7. capacity to influence physical or virtual environments.

This statement evaluates VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core against those elements.

## 5. Assessment Against EU AI Act Definition

### 5.1 Machine-Based System

The VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core may be documented, represented, or mechanically instantiated in software, formal notation, diagrams, repositories, publications, or audit artefacts.

However, the core itself is not defined as an operative software system.

It is a formal ontological framework.

A software implementation inspired by or derived from VECTAETOS™ must be assessed separately.

Therefore:

```text
VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core, as such,
is not treated here as a deployed machine-based operative system.
```

---

### 5.2 Autonomy

VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not operate with varying levels of autonomy.

It does not:

- select goals,
- choose objectives,
- initiate actions,
- execute tasks in the world,
- control other systems,
- act on behalf of a user,
- modify its own operational logic,
- autonomously adapt a policy,
- or pursue a target state.

Its formal role is structural and descriptive.

It exposes epistemic configuration.

It does not act.

Therefore, VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not satisfy the autonomy element of the AI system definition.

---

### 5.3 Adaptiveness After Deployment

VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not exhibit adaptiveness after deployment.

It does not contain:

- parameter updates,
- learning loops,
- reinforcement learning,
- adaptive policy updates,
- persistent self-modification,
- runtime optimization,
- model retraining,
- feedback-driven behavioural change,
- or post-deployment adaptation of the frozen ontological core.

Memory layers, if referenced in the broader architecture, are descriptive only and do not modify Φ, K(Φ), κ, QE, or the frozen ontological core.

Therefore, VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not satisfy the adaptiveness element of the AI system definition.

---

### 5.4 Inference From Input

VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not perform inference in the operational sense contemplated by the AI Act.

It does not:

- infer user intent for decision-making,
- infer latent variables for classification,
- infer predictions about real-world outcomes,
- infer recommendations,
- infer risk scores,
- infer behavioural profiles,
- infer compliance conclusions,
- infer policy actions,
- or infer automated operational outputs.

Any relational deformation, curvature expression, or projection is not an inference engine.

It is a structural representation of epistemic tension.

The framework does not approximate an external function, optimize a model, predict a future state, or infer a decision output.

Therefore, VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not satisfy the inference-based operational element of the AI system definition.

---

### 5.5 Explicit or Implicit Objectives

VECTAETOS™ 1.x has no explicit or implicit objective in the sense of target-seeking system behaviour.

It does not optimize toward:

- accuracy,
- utility,
- reward,
- engagement,
- conversion,
- compliance,
- classification success,
- risk minimization,
- behavioural influence,
- policy satisfaction,
- or decision outcome.

K(Φ) is not an objective function.

κ is not a tunable deployment threshold.

QE is not an error state to be minimized.

Projection is not output optimization.

The architecture is non-teleological.

Therefore, VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not satisfy the objective-driven element of the AI system definition.

---

### 5.6 Outputs: Predictions, Content, Recommendations, Decisions

VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not generate legally or operationally relevant outputs such as:

- predictions,
- recommendations,
- decisions,
- rankings,
- risk scores,
- classifications,
- compliance verdicts,
- behavioural instructions,
- or executable actions.

Its projections are structural and descriptive.

They do not carry decision authority.

They do not tell a user what to do.

They do not prescribe an outcome.

They do not rank alternatives.

They may legitimately remain silent.

Therefore, VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not satisfy the output element of an AI system in the operational sense.

---

### 5.7 Influence on Physical or Virtual Environments

VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not influence physical or virtual environments.

It does not:

- operate actuators,
- modify external systems,
- trigger automated actions,
- make operational choices,
- update third-party systems,
- control workflows,
- alter user rights,
- impose outcomes,
- or execute decisions.

Any influence would arise only from a downstream implementation, human interpretation, deployment wrapper, or third-party use.

Therefore, VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not itself satisfy the environmental influence element of the AI system definition.

## 6. Positioning Conclusion for Frozen Core

Based on the above technical assessment, the project position is:

```text
VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core is not designed, structured,
or operated as an AI system under the EU AI Act definition.
```

This conclusion is limited to the frozen ontological core.

It does not apply automatically to any downstream implementation.

It does not create a legal exemption for products, services, interfaces, APIs, dashboards, LLM wrappers, audit tools, or third-party deployments.

## 7. Downstream Implementation Boundary

### 7.1 General Boundary

The regulatory status of a downstream implementation depends on what that implementation actually does.

A downstream system may fall within the scope of the AI Act even if it is derived from, inspired by, named after, or structurally constrained by VECTAETOS™.

The following rule applies:

```text
Frozen Core positioning does not transfer automatically to downstream systems.
```

A VECTAETOS-derived implementation requires separate assessment if it includes any of the following:

- machine-learning inference,
- adaptive behaviour,
- autonomous operation,
- LLM-generated recommendations,
- automated compliance scoring,
- automated decision support,
- operational advice,
- risk classification,
- profiling,
- ranking,
- prediction,
- content generation,
- human-facing conclusions,
- integration into enterprise decision workflows,
- or capacity to influence physical or virtual environments.

---

### 7.2 ASIMULATOR

ASIMULATOR is a downstream procedural / simulation layer.

It is not identical with VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core.

If ASIMULATOR remains bounded research infrastructure, non-agentic, non-prescriptive, non-adaptive, and non-decisional, it may be positioned separately as structural simulation or procedural modelling infrastructure.

If ASIMULATOR becomes a deployed service that performs inference, generates recommendations, ranks alternatives, simulates operational outcomes for decision support, or influences user actions, it requires separate regulatory assessment.

ASIMULATOR must not be publicly represented as inheriting regulatory non-AI status from VECTAETOS™.

---

### 7.3 ASI_MOD

ASI_MOD is a downstream dialogic / interface / articulation layer.

It is not identical with VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core.

If ASI_MOD uses LLMs, natural-language generation, user-facing interpretation, summarization, memory, or interface-level guidance, it requires separate assessment.

ASI_MOD must not be framed as:

- a truth authority,
- a coherence authority,
- an automated decision interface,
- a compliance certifier,
- or a legal advisory system.

ASI_MOD must not inherit regulatory positioning from VECTAETOS™ automatically.

---

### 7.4 Simulation Vortex Services

The Simulation Vortex, in canonical VECTAETOS terms, is a non-agentic generator of candidate trajectories.

It must not select, rank, optimize, decide, recommend, or control outcomes.

If a Simulation Vortex service remains non-agentic, deterministic or bounded, non-adaptive, and descriptive only, it should be positioned as exploratory structural modelling rather than decision automation.

If it is deployed as a service that ranks trajectories, recommends paths, optimizes outcomes, predicts events, or triggers operational decisions, it requires separate assessment.

## 8. Epistemic Audit Interface / Epistemic Cryptography

### 8.1 Status

The Epistemic Audit Interface (EAI) and Epistemic Cryptography (EK) are downstream audit and structural-recording layers.

They are not identical with VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core.

They may implement VECTAETOS-derived constraints such as:

- non-intervention,
- no feedback,
- no optimization,
- deterministic structural extraction,
- cryptographic traceability,
- projection without interpretation,
- structural fingerprinting,
- reproducibility of audit artefacts.

### 8.2 Permitted Positioning

If implemented as deterministic, non-adaptive, non-prescriptive, non-ranking, non-classifying, non-decisional, and non-interventional audit tooling, EAI/EK may be positioned as:

```text
structural audit infrastructure
```

or:

```text
non-interventional structural traceability tooling
```

They may support:

- traceability,
- reproducibility,
- documentation,
- structural due diligence,
- audit evidence,
- tamper visibility,
- non-interventional inspection,
- and internal governance documentation.

### 8.3 Boundary

If EAI or EK are combined with any of the following, their regulatory status must be assessed separately:

- machine-learning inference,
- adaptive probing,
- automated risk scoring,
- compliance verdicts,
- LLM-generated recommendations,
- operational guidance,
- ranking or classification of persons,
- ranking or classification of entities,
- automated decision support,
- or integration into high-risk AI governance workflows.

### 8.4 Prohibited Claims

EAI/EK must not be represented as:

- a legal compliance guarantee,
- an official conformity assessment mechanism,
- an EU AI Act certification tool,
- a substitute for legal review,
- a substitute for human oversight,
- a substitute for risk management,
- a proof of real-world safety,
- a proof of empirical validity,
- or a legal verdict.

Canonical sentence:

```text
Structural audit evidence is not a legal verdict.
```

## 9. LLM / GPAI Boundary

VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core is not a general-purpose AI model.

It is not a foundation model.

It is not an LLM.

It is not trained, deployed, prompted, fine-tuned, or adapted as a generative AI model.

However, a downstream implementation may use an LLM or a general-purpose AI model as a linguistic adapter, interface, summarization layer, or operational wrapper.

If so, the LLM-based layer must be assessed independently.

The following rule applies:

```text
LLM use does not become non-AI merely because it is framed by VECTAETOS™ terminology.
```

Any LLM-based implementation may raise obligations or responsibilities under AI-related regulation depending on:

- the provider,
- the deployer,
- the intended purpose,
- the model used,
- the system architecture,
- the user-facing outputs,
- and the deployment context.

ASI_MOD, if LLM-based, must be treated as a separate downstream interface layer for regulatory analysis.

## 10. Commercial Deployment Boundary

Any commercial, SaaS, API, hosted interface, consulting tool, audit dashboard, or user-facing service built around VECTAETOS™ terminology must be assessed independently.

The following distinction applies:

```text
VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core = formal ontological framework
EAI / EK = possible structural audit implementation
ASIMULATOR = downstream procedural/simulation layer
ASI_MOD = downstream dialogic/interface layer
SaaS / API / dashboard = deployment wrapper
```

No deployment wrapper may inherit regulatory positioning merely because it references VECTAETOS™.

If a deployment wrapper generates:

- recommendations,
- predictions,
- classifications,
- rankings,
- risk scores,
- compliance verdicts,
- operational guidance,
- user-facing conclusions,
- automated reports with prescriptive meaning,
- or decision support outputs,

then it may fall within AI-related regulatory scope depending on implementation and intended use.

## 11. Prohibited AI Practices Boundary

VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core is not designed for, and must not be used to support:

- manipulative or deceptive techniques,
- exploitation of vulnerabilities,
- social scoring,
- biometric categorisation of persons,
- emotion recognition in prohibited contexts,
- predictive policing,
- unlawful biometric identification,
- profiling for punitive or discriminatory treatment,
- behavioural control,
- surveillance infrastructure,
- automated denial of rights,
- automated ranking or penalisation of persons,
- or any other prohibited AI practice under the AI Act.

Any third-party implementation that attempts to use VECTAETOS™ terminology or architecture for such purposes is outside the scope of this positioning and outside the legitimate project use.

## 12. AI Literacy Boundary

VECTAETOS-derived documentation, audit records, or structural explanations may support education, documentation, internal governance, or technical literacy.

However, VECTAETOS™ does not replace any organisation’s AI literacy obligations.

It does not train personnel by itself.

It does not certify AI literacy.

It does not satisfy organisational compliance duties automatically.

If VECTAETOS-derived tooling is used in an organisation that provides or deploys AI systems, that organisation remains responsible for its own AI literacy obligations and governance measures.

---

## 13. High-Risk AI Boundary

VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core is not itself a high-risk AI system.

It is not placed on the market as a product safety component.

It is not deployed as an AI system for high-risk use cases.

It does not perform high-risk functions such as:

- employment screening,
- access to education,
- access to essential services,
- creditworthiness assessment,
- law enforcement analysis,
- migration control,
- biometric identification,
- medical decision support,
- or safety-component control.

However, if a downstream implementation is integrated into or used with a high-risk AI system, that integration must be assessed independently.

A VECTAETOS-derived audit layer may support documentation or structural due diligence.

It must not be treated as automatic conformity assessment.
