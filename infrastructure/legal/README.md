## 1. Purpose

This directory exists to organize legal and regulatory orientation documents related to VECTAETOS.

- legal boundary notes,

- official regulatory approval,
- conformity assessment,
- certification,
- compliance verdicts,
- market-surveillance conclusions,

## 4. Relationship to VECTAETOS Core

The legal infrastructure layer is downstream from the ontological core.

Safe direction:

```text
VECTAETOS core → informs legal positioning
```

Forbidden reverse direction:

```text
legal positioning → mutates Φ
legal positioning → redefines Σ
legal positioning → redefines R
legal positioning → changes K(Φ)
legal positioning → changes κ
legal positioning → turns VECTAETOS into an AI system or non-AI shield
```

Legal documents may describe how VECTAETOS is positioned.

They may not change what VECTAETOS is.

## 9. Legal Review Triggers

A new legal review is required if any VECTAETOS-derived layer:

- becomes publicly deployed,
- becomes commercial,
- uses an LLM,
- generates user-facing content,
- produces reports,
- produces recommendations,
- ranks, scores, classifies, profiles, predicts, or advises,
- enters a regulated sector,
- is used by a public authority,
- is used in employment, education, credit, insurance, migration, law enforcement, healthcare, infrastructure, product safety, or essential services,
- processes personal data,
- processes sensitive data,
- processes biometric data,
- processes security-sensitive logs,
- processes confidential or trade-secret material,
- claims compliance,
- claims certification,
- claims safety,
- claims conformity,
- claims deployment readiness,
- claims EU AI Act positioning beyond the Frozen Core.

If any trigger applies, mark the artefact:

```text
LEGAL REVIEW REQUIRED
```

## 11. File Inclusion Criteria

A file belongs in `/infrastructure/legal/` if it concerns:

- regulatory positioning,
- public legal disclaimers,
- legal-status boundaries,
- compliance-facing public claims,
- trademark and identity policy, if not placed under `/governance`,
- EU / national / sectoral regulatory monitoring,
- legal-review triggers,
- non-reliance language,
- downstream implementation assessment boundaries.

A file does not belong here if it is:

- formal ontology,
- metamathematics,
- core theory,
- prototype design,
- runtime implementation,
- technical API documentation,
- commercial pitch,
- investor deck,
- military operational document,
- legal advice from counsel requiring confidentiality.
