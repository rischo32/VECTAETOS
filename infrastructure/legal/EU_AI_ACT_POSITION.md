## 3. Regulatory Reference: EU AI Act Definition of AI System

Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 defines an AI system as a machine-based system designed to operate with varying levels of autonomy, which may exhibit adaptiveness after deployment, and which, for explicit or implicit objectives, infers from input how to generate outputs such as predictions, content, recommendations, or decisions that can influence physical or virtual environments.

For positioning purposes, the relevant definitional elements are:

1. machine-based system,
2. varying levels of autonomy,
3. adaptiveness after deployment,
4. inference from input,
5. explicit or implicit objectives,
6. generation of outputs such as predictions, content, recommendations, or decisions,
7. capacity to influence physical or virtual environments.

This statement evaluates VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core against those elements.

This evaluation is a technical-regulatory positioning exercise. It does not convert the AI Act definition into a mechanical checklist and does not replace case-specific legal analysis. A downstream implementation may fall within scope even if the Frozen Core itself remains outside the operational AI-system role.

## 5. Assessment Against EU AI Act Definition

### 5.1 Machine-Based System

The VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core may be documented, represented, or mechanically instantiated in software, formal notation, diagrams, repositories, publications, or audit artefacts.

However, the core itself is not defined as an operative software system.

It is a formal ontological framework.

A software implementation inspired by or derived from VECTAETOS™ must be assessed separately.

The decisive distinction is not whether VECTAETOS™ concepts can be represented in software, but whether a specific artefact operates as a machine-based system that performs operational inference, generates user-facing outputs, pursues explicit or implicit objectives, or influences a physical or virtual environment.

The Frozen Ontological Core, as a formal specification, does none of these.

Any executable artefact implementing, simulating, visualising, wrapping, exposing, auditing, translating, or commercialising the Core must be assessed separately.

Therefore:

```text
VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core, as such,
is not treated here as a deployed machine-based operative system.
```

---

### 5.2 Autonomy

VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not operate with varying levels of autonomy.

It does not:

- select goals,
- choose objectives,
- initiate actions,
- execute tasks in the world,
- control other systems,
- act on behalf of a user,
- modify its own operational logic,
- autonomously adapt a policy,
- or pursue a target state.

Its formal role is structural and descriptive.

It exposes epistemic configuration.

It does not act.

Therefore, VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not satisfy the autonomy element of the AI system definition.

---

### 5.3 Adaptiveness After Deployment

VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not exhibit adaptiveness after deployment.

It does not contain:

- parameter updates,
- learning loops,
- reinforcement learning,
- adaptive policy updates,
- persistent self-modification,
- runtime optimization,
- model retraining,
- feedback-driven behavioural change,
- or post-deployment adaptation of the frozen ontological core.

Memory layers, if referenced in the broader architecture, are descriptive only and do not modify Φ, K(Φ), κ, QE, or the frozen ontological core.

Therefore, VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not satisfy the adaptiveness element of the AI system definition.

---

### 5.4 Inference From Input

VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not perform inference in the operational sense contemplated by the AI Act.

It does not:

- infer user intent for decision-making,
- infer latent variables for classification,
- infer predictions about real-world outcomes,
- infer recommendations,
- infer risk scores,
- infer behavioural profiles,
- infer compliance conclusions,
- infer policy actions,
- or infer automated operational outputs.

Any relational deformation, curvature expression, or projection is not an inference engine.

It is a structural representation of epistemic tension.

The framework does not approximate an external function, optimize a model, predict a future state, or infer a decision output.

This statement does not claim that every mathematical, symbolic, structural, ontological, or knowledge-based mapping is outside the AI Act.

It claims only that the Frozen Ontological Core does not perform operational inference from user input for the purpose of generating predictions, recommendations, decisions, classifications, rankings, compliance conclusions, behavioural guidance, or environment-influencing outputs.

Therefore, VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not satisfy the inference-based operational element of the AI system definition.

---

### 5.5 Explicit or Implicit Objectives

VECTAETOS™ 1.x has no explicit or implicit objective in the sense of target-seeking system behaviour.

It does not optimize toward:

- accuracy,
- utility,
- reward,
- engagement,
- conversion,
- compliance,
- classification success,
- risk minimization,
- behavioural influence,
- policy satisfaction,
- or decision outcome.

K(Φ) is not an objective function.

κ is not a tunable deployment threshold.

QE is not an error state to be minimized.

Projection is not output optimization.

The architecture is non-teleological.

Therefore, VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not satisfy the objective-driven element of the AI system definition.

---

### 5.6 Outputs: Predictions, Content, Recommendations, Decisions

VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not generate legally or operationally relevant outputs such as:

- predictions,
- recommendations,
- decisions,
- rankings,
- risk scores,
- classifications,
- compliance verdicts,
- behavioural instructions,
- or executable actions.

Its projections are structural and descriptive.

They do not carry decision authority.

They do not tell a user what to do.

They do not prescribe an outcome.

They do not rank alternatives.

They may legitimately remain silent.

The reference to “content” in the AI Act is relevant for downstream generative systems.

The Frozen Ontological Core is not a content-generation service.

Any LLM adapter, chatbot, summarisation layer, report generator, dashboard, documentation assistant, natural-language interface, compliance narrative generator, or public-facing explanation service that generates user-facing content must be assessed separately.

Therefore, VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not satisfy the output element of an AI system in the operational sense.

---

### 5.7 Influence on Physical or Virtual Environments

VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not influence physical or virtual environments.

It does not:

- operate actuators,
- modify external systems,
- trigger automated actions,
- make operational choices,
- update third-party systems,
- control workflows,
- alter user rights,
- impose outcomes,
- or execute decisions.

Any influence would arise only from a downstream implementation, human interpretation, deployment wrapper, or third-party use.

Therefore, VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not itself satisfy the environmental influence element of the AI system definition.

```text
Frozen Core positioning does not transfer automatically to downstream systems.
```

- adaptive behaviour,

- operational advice,
- risk classification,
- profiling,
- ranking,

- human-facing conclusions,

## 9A. Article 50 Transparency / Synthetic Content Boundary

Article 50 transparency obligations are a future-facing implementation boundary for any VECTAETOS-derived interface that directly interacts with natural persons or generates, manipulates, summarises, labels, marks, publishes, or explains content through AI.

This section does not apply to the Frozen Ontological Core as such.

It may apply to downstream layers such as:

```text
ASI_MOD
LLM adapters
chat interfaces
public explanation tools
report generators
summarisation services
audit dashboards using generated narratives
synthetic-content labelling tools
documentation assistants
interactive compliance walkthroughs
```

### 9A.1 Human Interaction Disclosure

If a downstream VECTAETOS-derived interface is an AI system that directly interacts with natural persons, it may need to ensure that persons are informed that they are interacting with an AI system.

The Frozen Core has no conversational agency and no human-facing interaction.

ASI_MOD or any LLM-based wrapper may have one.

### 9A.2 AI-Generated Content Marking

If a downstream system generates or manipulates audio, image, video, or text content, it may trigger transparency, marking, labelling, or detectability obligations depending on the implementation and use context.

A formal projection artefact is not automatically AI-generated public content.

A generated report, chatbot answer, public explanation, synthetic visual, generated glyph rendering, generated legal note, or generated compliance narrative may be.

### 9A.3 Deep Fakes, Public-Interest Text, Emotion Recognition, Biometric Categorisation

VECTAETOS-derived systems must not be deployed to conceal or launder the artificial nature of:

- deep fakes,
- AI-generated or manipulated public-interest publications,
- emotion-recognition outputs,
- biometric categorisation outputs,
- or synthetic content presented as authentic human-generated material.

### 9A.4 Transparency Code and Guidelines Watch

The European Commission and AI Office are developing and consulting on transparency guidance and a code of practice for marking and labelling AI-generated content.

Until final instruments are adopted or confirmed, VECTAETOS-derived implementations should treat these as live regulatory horizon items.

Implementation rule:

```text
If a downstream layer speaks to humans or generates content, Article 50 must be checked.
```

Canonical sentence:

```text
The Frozen Core is not a generative content service; downstream generative interfaces may be.
```

This section does not change the position that VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core is not itself a high-risk AI system.

Public EU materials following the 7 May 2026 political agreement indicate an adjusted high-risk implementation horizon:

```text
Annex III / stand-alone high-risk areas:
latest public horizon: 2 December 2027

AI systems embedded into regulated products / Union harmonisation legislation:
latest public horizon: 2 August 2028
```

These dates must be treated as horizon dates subject to final legal text, formal adoption, publication, and any implementing/delegated acts.

### 13A.2 Standards and Presumption of Conformity

Harmonised standards remain voluntary.

Where harmonised standards are referenced in the Official Journal of the European Union, their use may provide a presumption of conformity with corresponding legal requirements.

VECTAETOS-derived tooling must not claim conformity with an AI Act harmonised standard unless:

- the relevant standard is final,
- the standard is referenced in the Official Journal where required,
- the implementation actually falls within its scope,
- the implementation demonstrably applies it,
- and the claim is verified through appropriate legal and technical review.

A VECTAETOS-derived audit layer must not use standardisation language to imply legal approval.

Forbidden claims include:

```text

EAI/EK creates presumption of conformity.
Structural traceability equals Article 17 quality management.
A glyph, projection, fingerprint, or audit artefact satisfies technical documentation.
```

unless a specific downstream implementation has been separately assessed and the claim is legally and technically supportable.

### 13A.4 Common Specifications / Commission Guidance / Support Tools

If harmonised standards, common specifications, templates, Commission guidance, AI Office guidance, Service Desk tools, conformity-assessment guidance, or sectoral implementation acts become applicable, downstream systems must be re-assessed.

The Frozen Core remains formal and non-agentic, but deployment wrappers may acquire obligations from the support-tool environment around the AI Act.

A VECTAETOS-derived project may use a sandbox only as an innovation, testing, or regulatory-learning environment.

Sandbox participation must not be represented as:

- product-safety overlap,
- machinery-sector overlap,

```text
No VECTAETOS layer may become infrastructure for synthetic sexual abuse, non-consensual intimate content, or child sexual abuse material.
```

## 18. Final Position

VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core is a formal, non-agentic, non-teleological, non-adaptive, non-prescriptive, non-decisional ontological framework.

It exposes structure.

It does not decide.

It does not recommend.

It does not optimize.

It does not classify persons.

It does not score compliance.

It does not certify safety.

It does not influence physical or virtual environments.

Accordingly, the project position is that the frozen core is not designed, structured, or operated as an AI system under the EU AI Act definition.

This position is limited to the frozen core only.

All downstream implementations remain separately assessable.
