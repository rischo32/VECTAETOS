the spectral extension analyzes the **geometric modes of relational tension**.

# 1. Spectral Interpretation of Relational Observables

From the canonical layer, relational observables form an antisymmetric matrix:

A = [Aᵢⱼ]

defined for node pairs:

(i, j), i ≠ j

such that:

Aᵢⱼ = −Aⱼᵢ

Therefore:

Aᵀ = −A

This embeds the relational observable matrix in the Lie algebra:

A ∈ so(n)

where:

so(n) = set of n×n antisymmetric real matrices.

Interpretation:

Relational tensions act as **generators of rotational symmetry**
in an abstract relational space.

# 2. Spectral Decomposition

Define the eigen-spectrum of A:

λ₁, λ₂, …, λₙ

such that:

A v = λ v

Properties of antisymmetric matrices:

- eigenvalues occur in pairs
- eigenvalues are purely imaginary

Formally:

λ = ± iω

where ω ∈ ℝ.

Interpretation:

Spectral modes represent **structural rotations of relational tension**.

They do not represent gradients or optimization flows.

# 8. Triality Structure

Three fundamental representations exist:

V⁸   — vector representation  
S⁺⁸  — positive spinor representation  
S⁻⁸  — negative spinor representation  

These are related by an outer automorphism:

τ : V⁸ → S⁺⁸ → S⁻⁸ → V⁸

Triality is a symmetry of order three.

Interpretation:

No single representation may dominate the relational structure.
