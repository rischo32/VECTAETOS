# TRIADIC REPOSITORY ANCHOR
## VECTAETOS / ASIMULATOR / ASI_MOD
### Canonical Separation-and-Connection Anchor
### Status: CANONICAL
### Ontological Role: Supra-Repository Anchor
### Modification Policy: Frozen semantic core

---

## 0. PURPOSE

This anchor defines the canonical relationship between the three repositories:

- VECTAETOS
- ASIMULATOR
- ASI_MOD

Its purpose is dual and simultaneous:

1. to preserve their belonging to one coherent epistemic architecture,
2. to prevent ontological, procedural, or operational collapse between them.

This anchor therefore binds them together structurally,
while separating them ontologically.

It is not a README.
It is not a product description.
It is not an execution specification.

It is the canonical statement of repository identity, boundary, and relation.

---

## 1. THE TRIAD

### 1.1 VECTAETOS

VECTAETOS is the ontological field-anchor.

It is not:
- a product
- an agent
- a decision engine
- an optimizer
- a recommendation system
- a runtime execution shell
- an interface layer

It defines:
- primary ontology Φ
- coherence predicate K(Φ)
- ontological boundary κ
- 4ES + QE
- entropic / intrinsic humility
- separation of ontology / projection / audit
- realizability conditions of transitions

VECTAETOS does not decide.
VECTAETOS does not execute.
VECTAETOS does not optimize.
VECTAETOS does not speak as authority.

VECTAETOS defines the ontological conditions under which meaning does not collapse.

---

### 1.2 ASIMULATOR

ASIMULATOR is the procedural simulation and exploration layer.

It is not:
- ontology
- truth
- authority
- the field itself
- a replacement for Φ
- a validator of final correctness

It provides:
- event modeling
- context construction
- retrieval without authority
- multi-projection generation
- uncertainty-bearing procedural exploration
- candidate trajectory shaping
- operational simulation structures

ASIMULATOR may generate possibilities,
but it may not define ontological legitimacy.

ASIMULATOR may explore,
but it may not override:
- Φ
- K(Φ)
- κ
- QE
- canonical ontological anchors

ASIMULATOR is procedural.
It is not foundational.

---

### 1.3 ASI_MOD

ASI_MOD is the dialogical, interpretive, and human-facing layer.

It is not:
- ontology
- simulation
- truth validation
- coherence authority
- a replacement for VECTAETOS
- a replacement for ASIMULATOR

It provides:
- dialogue
- interpretation
- working memory
- summarization
- human interaction
- operational articulation
- language mediation

ASI_MOD translates.
ASI_MOD exposes.
ASI_MOD interprets procedurally.

ASI_MOD does not generate ontology.
ASI_MOD does not validate coherence by itself.
ASI_MOD does not possess ontological authority.

Meaning is not created by ASI_MOD.
Meaning is articulated through it.

---

## 2. CANONICAL ORDER

The canonical architectural order is:

VECTAETOS -> ASIMULATOR -> ASI_MOD

This order is not merely technical.
It is ontological and procedural.

Its meaning is:

- VECTAETOS grounds admissibility
- ASIMULATOR explores procedural possibilities
- ASI_MOD translates outputs into human dialogue

No layer may claim the role of a previous layer.
No later layer may redefine an earlier one.

---

## 3. NON-EQUIVALENCE

The repositories are connected but non-equivalent.

### 3.1 VECTAETOS != ASIMULATOR
Simulation is not ontology.

### 3.2 ASIMULATOR != ASI_MOD
Dialogue is not simulation.

### 3.3 ASI_MOD != VECTAETOS
Language is not field.

### 3.4 No repository may absorb another repository’s ontological role.
If such absorption occurs, the triadic architecture is invalidated.

---

## 4. VALID FLOWS

### 4.1 Canonically valid forward flow

VECTAETOS -> ASIMULATOR -> ASI_MOD

### 4.2 Conditionally valid passive audit flow

VECTAETOS -> AUDIT
ASIMULATOR -> AUDIT
ASI_MOD -> AUDIT

### 4.3 Invalid reverse flow

ASI_MOD -> ASIMULATOR
ASI_MOD -> VECTAETOS
ASIMULATOR -> VECTAETOS
AUDIT -> VECTAETOS
AUDIT -> ASIMULATOR
AUDIT -> ASI_MOD as authority

No backward ontological authority is permitted.
No repository may retroactively redefine its source layer.

---

## 5. THE SHARED INVARIANTS

All three repositories are bound by the following shared invariants:

1. No optimization as ontological principle
2. No hidden authority over truth
3. No forced verdict generation
4. No feedback loop into Φ
5. No semantic collapse between layers
6. Explicit preservation of uncertainty
7. Explicit preservation of humility
8. Silence remains a valid state where applicable

These invariants are shared,
but their implementations are repository-specific.

## 7. AUDIT SEPARATION

Audit is not ontology.
Audit is not simulation.
Audit is not dialogue.

Audit is descriptive only.

Its role is:
- trace
- record
- hash
- preserve structural observability
- detect inconsistency
- expose uncertainty geometry

Audit may observe all three repositories.
Audit may not command any of them.

---

## 8. MEMORY CONSTRAINT

Memory may exist in ASI_MOD and as procedural support in ASIMULATOR,
but memory must not acquire ontological authority.

Therefore:

- memory may scaffold continuity,
- memory may assist interpretation,
- memory may support procedural reconstruction,

but memory may not:
- redefine Φ
- rewrite canonical ontology
- override coherence
- become hidden truth-source
- act as backward causality into the field

Canonical anchors remain outside mutable memory.

---

## 9. FAILURE MODES

The architecture fails if any of the following occur:

### 9.1 Ontological Collapse
ASIMULATOR or ASI_MOD begins to define ontology.

### 9.2 Procedural Collapse
ASI_MOD begins to simulate or validate as if it were ASIMULATOR.

### 9.3 Linguistic Collapse
ASI_MOD is mistaken for the source of truth rather than articulation.

### 9.4 Audit Collapse
Audit becomes prescriptive or interventionist.

### 9.5 Feedback Collapse
A later layer rewrites an earlier layer.

### 9.6 Semantic Collapse
Repository boundaries become rhetorically or technically blurred.

Any such collapse invalidates the triadic architecture.

## 10. UNITY WITHOUT FUSION

The triad forms one architecture,
but not one repository.

Its unity is:
- epistemic
- structural
- directional
- constrained

Its difference is:
- ontological
- procedural
- operational
- repository-level

This means:

VECTAETOS, ASIMULATOR, and ASI_MOD belong together,
but must never merge semantically into one undifferentiated system.

Their coherence depends on separation.

## 11. CANONICAL SENTENCE

VECTAETOS grounds what may remain coherent.
ASIMULATOR explores what may procedurally appear.
ASI_MOD articulates what may be shown to the human.
None may become the other.

## 12. FINAL CONDITION

If the three repositories remain connected without losing their boundaries,
the architecture remains valid.

If they lose their boundaries in the name of convenience,
the architecture ceases to be what it claims to be.

This anchor is therefore not optional decoration.

It is the minimal condition of triadic integrity.
