## 0. Purpose

This document defines the regulatory position of **VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core** in relation to Regulation (EU) 2024/1689, known as the EU Artificial Intelligence Act.

The purpose of this statement is to clarify that VECTAETOS™ 1.x, as formally defined in its frozen ontological lineage, is not designed, structured, or operated as an AI system, decision system, recommendation engine, autonomous agent, optimization mechanism, or operational authority.

This assessment applies strictly to the frozen VECTAETOS™ 1.x ontological core.

It does not automatically apply to downstream, future, experimental, procedural, simulation, interface, or deployment layers.

2. Regulatory Reference: EU AI Act Definition

Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 defines an AI system by reference to a machine-based system designed to operate with varying levels of autonomy, which may exhibit adaptiveness after deployment, and which, for explicit or implicit objectives, infers from input how to generate outputs such as predictions, content, recommendations, or decisions that can influence physical or virtual environments.

For regulatory positioning purposes, the key definitional elements are:

    1. machine-based system
    2. varying levels of autonomy
    3. adaptiveness after deployment
    4. inference from input
    5. explicit or implicit objectives
    6. generation of predictions, content, recommendations, or decisions
    7. capacity to influence physical or virtual environments

This statement evaluates VECTAETOS™ 1.x against these elements.

4. Assessment Against EU AI Act Definition
4.1 Autonomy

VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not operate with varying levels of autonomy.

It does not:

    select goals
    choose objectives
    modify its operational logic
    initiate actions
    execute tasks in the world
    control other systems
    act on behalf of a user

Its formal role is structural and descriptive.

It exposes epistemic configuration.

It does not act.

Therefore, VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not satisfy the autonomy element of the AI system definition.

4.2 Adaptiveness After Deployment

VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not exhibit adaptiveness after deployment.

It does not contain:

    parameter updates
    learning loops
    reinforcement learning
    adaptive policy updates
    persistent self-modification
    runtime optimization
    model retraining
    feedback-driven behavioral change

Memory layers, if referenced in the broader architecture, are descriptive only and do not modify Φ, K(Φ), κ, or the frozen ontological core.

Therefore, VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not satisfy the adaptiveness element of the AI system definition.

4.3 Inference

VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not perform inference in the operational sense contemplated by the EU AI Act.

It does not:

    infer user intent for decision-making
    infer latent variables for classification
    infer predictions about real-world outcomes
    infer recommendations
    infer risk scores
    infer behavioral profiles
    infer policy actions

Any internal relational deformation or projection is not an inference engine.

It is a structural representation of epistemic tension.

The framework does not approximate an external function, optimize a model, predict a future state, or infer a decision output.

Therefore, VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not satisfy the inference-based operational element of the AI system definition.

4.4 Explicit or Implicit Objectives

VECTAETOS™ 1.x has no explicit or implicit objective in the sense of target-seeking system behavior.

It does not optimize toward:

    accuracy
    utility
    reward
    engagement
    conversion
    classification success
    risk minimization
    behavioral influence
    policy compliance
    decision outcome

K(Φ) is not an objective function.

κ is not a tunable threshold for deployment.

QE is not an error state to be minimized.

Projection is not output optimization.

The architecture is non-teleological.

Therefore, VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not satisfy the objective-directed behavior element of the AI system definition.

4.5 Output Type

VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not generate legally operative outputs such as:

    predictions
    recommendations
    decisions
    rankings
    scores
    classifications of persons
    behavioral guidance
    automated determinations
    commands
    actions

Projection output may be empty.

Silence is a valid structural outcome.

A projection is not a recommendation.

A projection is not a decision.

A projection is not an instruction.

A projection is not an automated action.

Therefore, VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not satisfy the output element of the AI system definition.

4.6 Influence on Physical or Virtual Environments

VECTAETOS™ 1.x has no capacity to influence physical or virtual environments.

It does not:

    control devices
    modify external systems
    execute transactions
    trigger workflows
    alter user accounts
    rank users
    approve or deny access
    change legal status
    affect employment, education, credit, insurance, healthcare, policing, migration, or public services

Any real-world action taken by a person or external system after reading a VECTAETOS™ projection is outside the ontological and operational scope of VECTAETOS™.

Responsibility remains with the human actor, implementer, deployer, or external system.

Therefore, VECTAETOS™ 1.x does not satisfy the environmental influence element of the AI system definition.

It is a fixed, non-agentic, non-adaptive, non-optimizing relational epistemic field framework.

Accordingly, VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core should be positioned as outside the definition of an AI system under the EU AI Act.
