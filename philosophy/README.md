# PHILOSOPHY

## Philosophical and Ontological Layer of VECTAETOS

### Status
Conceptual layer  
Non-formal  
Non-executive  
Non-authoritative  
Upstream of research, but not above formal anchors  

---

## 0. Purpose

The `/philosophy` directory contains the philosophical, ontological, and conceptual material of VECTAETOS.

It is a place for:

- theory,
- reflections,
- conceptual origins,
- meaning structures,
- philosophical notes,
- ontological distinctions,
- pre-formal formulations,
- interpretive essays.

This directory exists to preserve the thinking from which formal structures may later be clarified.

It is not the formal core.

It is not runtime logic.

It is not a decision layer.

It is not an authority over `/formal`.

---

## 1. Relation to `/formal`

The `/formal` directory contains canonical formal definitions.

The `/philosophy` directory may explain, illuminate, motivate, or expand the meaning around those definitions.

However, `/philosophy` must not silently redefine anything already frozen in `/formal`.

If a philosophical text conflicts with a canonical formal anchor, the formal anchor remains authoritative for the framework.

Philosophy may prepare future formalization.

It does not override existing formalization.

---

## 2. Relation to `/research`

The `/research` directory is for investigation, experiments, comparisons, exploratory mechanisms, technical hypotheses, and unfinished research artifacts.

The `/philosophy` directory is for conceptual grounding.

In simple terms:


/philosophy = why it means what it means
/research   = how it may be explored
/formal     = what is canonically defined

Research may draw from philosophy.
Formalization may later draw from research.
But no layer may bypass the canonical anchors.

## 3. Relation to /corpus

The /corpus directory contains curated epistemic fragments intended for structured reading, retrieval, or higher-layer interpretation.

The /philosophy directory may contain longer, rougher, deeper, and less curated source material.

In simple terms:

/philosophy = source thinking
/corpus     = curated fragments

A sentence may originate in /philosophy.
It may later be refined into /corpus.
But /corpus must remain descriptive and non-authoritative.

## 4. What Belongs Here

This directory may contain:

philosophical essays,
ontological notes,
reflections on truth, freedom, wisdom, reciprocity, existence, creation, unity, and intention,
conceptual sketches,
proto-formal thoughts,
historical development notes,
semantic distinctions,
explanatory texts around VECTAETOS,
human-readable theory.

## 5. What Does Not Belong Here

This directory must not contain:

executable runtime logic,
decision engines,
optimization functions,
scoring systems,
hidden policy layers,
formal redefinitions of Φ,
silent changes to 4ES + QE,
changes to κ,
changes to K(Φ),
changes to the frozen axiomatic structure.

Philosophy may think.
It may not command.

## 6. Boundary Rule

The philosophical layer is allowed to generate meaning.

It is not allowed to generate authority.

It may deepen interpretation.

It may not redefine ontology.

It may prepare language for future formalization.

It may not silently become formalism.
